Hidden Mor van Dashorst I.1 by Volker Hermes
Original work by Anthonis Mor van Dashorst, Mauritshuis, Netherlands (public domain)

The first thing that catches the eye is the enormous gold chain the sitter is wearing. I have extended the chain and let it run under his leather doublet. The design of the elaborate slits in the leather was twofold.

The perforations made it easier for the wearer to move in the stiff leather (practical), but of course, they gave a glimpse of what was underneath, opening up new possibilities for representation.

I have covered the sitter with parts of these leather slits to illustrate this point. Now, the outer garment is not the only clothing that can allude to one's wealth - everything underneath is also very precious.

So in the case of this young man, his military doublet is combined with the glittering, courtly fabric underneath. He is a strong figure but then also very posh.
